DANCE FORMS

Bharatnatyam
Bharatanatyam, an eminent Indian classical dance form presumably the oldest classical dance heritage of India is regarded as mother of many Indian classical dance forms.
Damhal
Damhal is the most popular folk dance of Kashmir. Men with conical caps and colorful robes perform this folk dance with a drum and singing. It is truly an amazing dance form.
Deodhani
Deodhani, a dance from Assam, is derived from two words, i.e. Deo means God and Dhani means woman, literally meaning a woman possessed (Shaman) by god.
Kathak
Kathak is one of the ancient Indian classical dance and is traditionally regarded to have originated from the travelling bards of North India referred as Kathakars or storytellers.
Bhangra
Bhangra is an energetic, folk dance and music form that originated from Punjab, India.The dance was associated primarily with the spring harvest festival Baisakhi
Kathakali
Kathakali, a dance form originated in Kerela which tells a story through elaborate and colourful makeup and face masks, performed by male traditionally actor-dancers.
